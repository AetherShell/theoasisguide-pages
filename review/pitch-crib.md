# Pitch crib sheet — The Oasis Guide

Phone-friendly. Glance before walking in.

---

## 30-second version

> Hi, I'm James Arkell, water professional based in Phoenix.
>
> I'm launching a small editorial guide called **The Oasis Guide**. It's a directory of Phoenix restaurants that serve clean water, verified in person with calibrated meters, readings published on each listing.
>
> Free for diners, free for restaurants, no pay-to-tier. Wanted to see if [Restaurant] would consider being a founding listing.

That's it. Stop and let them respond. Don't oversell.

---

## 2-minute version (if they engage)

**What it is:**
> An editorial directory. Phoenix-metro only, restaurants that actually filter or purify the water they serve. Every listing has the TDS and pH readings on the page, taken in person by me with calibrated meters at the bar, the ice machine, the drinking station, and the kitchen prep tap.

**Why it exists:**
> Phoenix water is hard. Most restaurants serve it straight from the tap. A few don't, and there's no good way for diners to find them. This is the directory I wished existed.

**The three tiers:**
> Recommended is purified drinking water and beverages. Distinguished adds ice and cooking water. Exemplary is whole-restaurant point-of-entry purification, which is rare. Recommended is the floor for the directory — if your readings don't reach it, the listing doesn't happen this round (no public listing either way; nothing negative goes up).

**What the visit looks like:**
> About 20 minutes. I bring the meters. I test every customer-facing tap, take photos for the listing, write up the readings. You see them before they go live and can flag anything sensitive.

**Founders perk:**
> First three to five founding agreements are recognized permanently in a founding-members section on the guide. Founding badge attaches to the agreement, not to the readings — even if your kit visit doesn't reach Recommended this round, the founding-cohort recognition stays. Founders whose readings also reach the directory also get a featured spot on the home page.

**The ask:**
> Twenty minutes for the on-site test this week or next. No fee, no contract, no exclusivity. If your readings come back clean you go on the directory. If they don't, nothing public happens.

## Objection handlers

**"What's it cost?"**
> Nothing. Free listing, free test, no contract. The tier you earn is the tier you display.

**"What if our water tests bad?"**
> Then you're not on the directory yet. Nothing public, no negative listing. I only publish places I can verify as clean. This applies to two cases: (1) the readings come back genuinely poor at every tap, and (2) the readings show you've filtered some things (cooking, equipment) but not the diner-facing stuff (drinking water, beverages) — that's below the Recommended floor and doesn't get a listing this round. In both cases, kit data stays private, the relationship continues, and the listing's available the moment the readings reach the floor.

**"How is this different from Yelp or Michelin or a best-of list?"**
> Those run on submissions and self-attestation. This runs on actual meter readings taken in person, with the receipts published. Diners can verify; restaurants can point to the readings instead of making claims.

**"What's in it for you?"**
> It's the directory I wished existed when I started looking for restaurants my family could trust. Founding listings stay permanent and free.

**"How long until you go live?"**
> Site is live at theoasisguide.com. First listings get verified in the coming days. Your listing publishes after your kit visit lands readings that reach Recommended.

**"What if we change our setup later?"**
> Annual re-test. If you invest in better filtration you can move up a tier. Nothing locks you in.

**"Who's behind this?"**
> Safe Water Southwest, the parent brand. I personally vet every listing on the guide.

**"Can I see what a listing looks like first?"**
> Yes. Theoasisguide.com has the empty directory and the tier explanations. The first listings are being verified now.

**"What if I'm part of a chain?"**
> Each location is verified separately. The directory lists locations, not brands.

**"Can I get a sticker / window decal?"**
> Yes. Printable decal and menu badge come with the listing, in your verified tier.

## Things to NOT say

(per the pitch-discipline lock)

- Don't mention paid Featured slots, vendor referrals, or any future monetization. If pushed on revenue model, say "long term the business model fills in, but founding listings stay permanent and free" and move on.
- Don't pitch Safe Water Southwest's residential angle to a restaurant audience. SWSW's "safe water" framing is for homeowners; for restaurants the framing is editorial recognition.
- Don't make health claims about alkaline or mineral water. Stick to "purified" and "filtered."
- Don't frame other restaurants as bad. The guide is about what is clean, not what isn't.
- Don't promise a listing before the kit visit. The kit visit determines whether readings reach the Recommended floor; the listing follows from the readings.
