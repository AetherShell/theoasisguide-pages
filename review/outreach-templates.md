# Restaurant outreach templates — The Oasis Guide

Founding-pitch framing only. **No mention of paid Featured slots, vendor referrals, or future monetization** — those are v2+. v1 pitch: free on-site test, free listing for restaurants whose readings reach the Recommended floor, founders' perk for being first.

**Floor rule in cold outreach:** the templates promise the *test*, not the *listing*. Restaurants whose readings don't reach Recommended (purified drinking water and beverages) don't get a directory listing — but the cold outreach doesn't lead with that; the bar conversation happens at the in-person pitch / kit-visit booking. Use the pitch-crib-sheet's "What if our water tests bad?" handler in that warmer setting. Frontloading disqualification in cold copy kills response rates without buying honesty (the bar gets explained the moment the kit visit is scheduled).

Each template runs ~120–160 words for email, ~70 words for text. Edit the bracketed bits per restaurant.

---

## 1. Cold email — generic Phoenix restaurant

> **Subject:** Phoenix water guide — invitation for [Restaurant name]

> Hi [Owner/Manager name],
>
> I'm James Arkell, a water professional based in Phoenix. I'm launching a
> small editorial guide — **The Oasis Guide** — for Phoenix restaurants that
> serve clean water. Verified in person, tap by tap, with the
> actual TDS and pH readings published on each listing.
>
> The on-site test is free. The listing is free — for restaurants whose
> readings reach the Recommended floor (purified drinking water and
> beverages). No contract, no pay-to-tier — the tier you display is the
> tier the meters earned. I'm writing because [specific reason — saw your
> RO setup / chef interview / menu mention of filtered water / friend
> referral / etc.].
>
> The first three to five founding agreements are recognized permanently in
> a founding-members section on the guide. Founders whose readings also
> reach the directory get a featured spot on the home page. The on-site
> visit takes about 20 minutes.
>
> theoasisguide.com goes live this week. Worth a 15-minute call to see if
> it fits?
>
> James Arkell
> Safe Water Southwest · The Oasis Guide
> [phone] · [email]

## What NOT to say (per pitch-discipline lock)

These phrases break the v1 framing and should not appear in any outreach until v2 launch:

- "Paid placement" / "Featured listing fee" / "Premium tier"
- "We can refer customers to your installer" (vendor referrals are v2)
- "Industry partnership" / "Sponsorship" (commercial-flavored language)
- "Best of Phoenix" awards language (we're a directory, not an awards body)
- Any health claims about alkaline / mineral water (credibility risk)
- "Safe water" framing toward the restaurant (fear-based; doesn't fit
  editorial voice — reserved for residential SWSW lead-gen)
- "You'll be on the directory" or "we'll list you" before the kit visit
  has confirmed Recommended-floor readings. The test is free; the listing
  follows the readings, not the agreement.
