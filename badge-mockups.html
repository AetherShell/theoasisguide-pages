The Oasis Guide — Badge Mockups

Three directions for the window decal / menu badge. Pick one, mix elements, or kill all three and we go again.

Option A — Editorial Seal (Michelin / James Beard flavor)

Classic circular guide-seal. Reads as authority. Restaurants are familiar with this shape from food awards.

Window decal — on glass

3" diameter on a glass door. Dark teal vinyl + gold metallic ink. Premium feel.

Menu badge — printed on paper

Same mark, paper-friendly single color. Sits next to drink prices on the menu.

Tier variants — three states

Same seal, different drop count + tier name. Customers learn the language fast.

Option B — Editorial Wordmark (minimalist)

Pure typography. No seal, no icon. Feels like a New Yorker masthead. Hardest to ignore on a clean menu.

Window decal — horizontal

Long horizontal sticker for the corner of a glass door. 8" wide.

Menu line — stacked

Stacked block at the foot of the menu next to "spirits" or "kitchen team."

Three tiers — wordmark only

No seal — just the wordmark and the tier line. Most editorial, least "badge-y."

Option C — Modern Geometric (oasis as concept)

Hexagonal frame, water-drop motif, tier integrated into the sticker. Each restaurant gets the decal matching their earned tier — that's what goes on the glass.

Recommended — window decal

Single drop. Bronze tier. The "we take water seriously" floor.

Distinguished — window decal

Two drops. Silver tier. Kitchen + bar + ice all clean.

Exemplary — window decal

Three drops. Gold tier. Whole-restaurant clean. The unicorn.

Menu mark — single color

Same shape, single-color version for menus and print.

Tier variants

Hex form with tier marks. The most "branded" feel of the three options.

How to compare them

Imagine each on the actual surface where it'll live:

  • A — Editorial Seal: Looks like Michelin / James Beard / AAA. Authoritative. The "safest" choice. Risk: looks generic next to other seals if you're not careful with the color palette.
  • B — Wordmark only: Cleanest. The pull-quote of the three. Feels like a magazine masthead. Risk: less recognizable at a glance from across the street.
  • C — Hex geometric: Most ownable visually. Doesn't look like anyone else. Risk: a touch trendy — could date faster than A or B.

My pick: Option A for the window decal (authority where strangers see it) + Option B for the menu badge (typographic restraint where the diner is already seated). Best of both. Lets the seal do the work outside, the wordmark do the work inside.
